The secret to the FIR filter's power lies in its intricate design, which involves carefully tuning a set of coefficients to precisely match the characteristics of the input signal. These coefficients are then multiplied by delayed versions of the input signal, creating a web of interlocking signals that would make even the most seasoned engineer's head spin.

But don't let the complexity of the FIR filter intimidate you. With a little patience, perseverance, and a generous helping of caffeine, you too can master the art of FIR filter design and unlock the full potential of your audio signals. Just remember to keep a spare calculator handy, and never underestimate the power of a good pair of noise-cancelling headphones."

In this equation shows a simple FIR filter. The coefficients b[k] are the filter taps, and x[n-k] represents the input sample delayed by k time steps. The sum of the product of the filter taps and the delayed input samples produces the output sample y[n] at time n.

$$ \begin{align*} y[n] = \sum_{k=0}^{M-1} b[k] * x[n-k] \\ \end{align*}$$
